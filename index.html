Connecting America: The 21 Airline Companies and The Ones You Should Choose

By Nilay Menon

In the United States, air travel is very common as there were about 7 million flights that have been taken in the year 2022. People are constantly on the move and using the sky as means for travel and transportation. During 2022, travelers were able to choose from over 21 different airline companies when it came to domestic air travel. Now, what causes travelers to choose certain airline companies over others? We will look into certain data like cancellation rates for popular airline companies, average delay times for each airline and even routes of popular airline companies to see what airline companies are consumers using.

Seaonal Trends In Domestic Air Travel


One of the number one things we like to know is wheter the airline company we are choosing can get us from point A to point B safetly and on time. Being punctional is a big plus that travelers expect from airlines. If travelers cant be sure of the reliabilty of an airline in terms of delays, what would make them chose such airline company? So lets examine the airlines and their average delay trends in minutes for each month for the year 2022.

If you hover over each line, you are able to see which airline company the line represents and their average delay time in minutes for the whole year



There are a couple of things to take away from this line plot. As we can see there appears to be a certain trends for almost every airline during certain months. Between the months of March and July, there is a increase in delay times. This could be due to things like spring and summer break for students which can cause increase in airline demands and staff. The other trend we can see is that between the months July and September, we can see a decline in delays potentially due to summer vacations ending and students starting to go back to school. This decline in delays doesnt last long as winter time is coming up and holidays like Thanksgiving, Christmas and New Year's bring more delays due to an increase in demands from the airline. Of course, we can see some potential outliers in these trends. For example, JetBlue has an abnormally high average delay time. This could have been due to higher number of failures within the airline within ground control or even aircraft failures before takeoff. Certain airlines companies like Piedmont and Horizon are subsidaries to major airline companies like American Airline and Alaska Airlines. The number of flights these subsidary companies might serve could be less which can attribute to less delays
